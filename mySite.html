My Career

...As a Professional

My career as a Computer Scientist began in 2013, when I started making mods for the PC version of the game 'Skyrim'. This gave me my first experience with modelling tools, scripting, game design, etc. I eventually made many mods that I published onto the Steam Workshop for download, and they now have a cumulative of over 100,000 downloads. I had a lot of fun making these mods, so when I saw people care about them and become popular, I knew that this was what I wanted to do with my life. Find ways to give people better experiences through software. There is something immensely satisfying to me about identifying a problem, and fixing it to make a product better.

"...I knew that this was what I wanted to do with my life..."

My education in coding began in my senior year of high school, where I learned the basics of computer science and solidified my thoughts that this was the field for me. In Fall of 2016, I began college at West Virginia University, where I am studying to receive a B.S. in Computer Science, as well as a minor in Entrepreneurial Studies. In my time at college, I have been lucky enough to have several opportunities in industry, including designing an ecommerce website for a multimillion dollar company with Myers Carpet, working with the Naval Nuclear Labs on a project to increase resource allocation efficiency, and my most recent employment opportunity, working as a Development Support Intern with NextGen Federal Systems where I get to work with full time engineers on a full stack project to create a web app for internal use.
