...As a Professional

My career as a Software Engineer began in 2013, when I started making mods for the PC version of the game 'Skyrim'. This gave me my first experience with modelling tools, scripting, game design, etc. I eventually made many mods that I published onto the Steam Workshop for public download, where they now have more than 100,000 combined downloads. I had a lot of fun making these mods, mostly from learning the technologies needed to fix flaws in a game I care a lot about. This experience with improving software taught me that this was what I wanted to do with my career.

My formal education in programming began in my senior year of high school, where I learned the basics of computer science and solidified my thoughts that this was the field for me. In fall of 2016, I began college at West Virginia University, where I went on to receive a B.S. in Computer Science, as well as a minor in Entrepreneurial Studies.

After graduating in December 2019, I have been working as an Associate Software Engineer for nCino, where I have been honing my technical skills and growing professionally.
